The Health Score (0–100) is a weighted composite of 8 financial ratios drawn from sector benchmarks and practitioner standards. Each ratio is rated Good, Moderate, or Poor based on established nonprofit financial thresholds. Good = full weight, Moderate = half weight, Poor = zero weight.
Ratio Weight Good Moderate Poor
Months of Cash 20% ≥ 3 months 1.5 – 3 months < 1.5 months
Operating Margin 18% ≥ 7% 0% – 7% < 0% (deficit)
Program Expense Ratio 15% ≥ 65% 55% – 65% < 55%
Current Ratio 12% ≥ 1.5× 1.0× – 1.5× < 1.0×
Revenue Concentration 10% ≤ 40% 40% – 60% > 60%
Debt to Assets 10% ≤ 40% 40% – 65% > 65%
Fundraising Efficiency 8% ≤ 20% 20% – 35% > 35%
Net Asset Ratio 7% ≥ 0.25× 0.1× – 0.25× < 0.1×
Score reflects current year data only. Revenue Concentration is scored as Good when the largest source is a broad base of individual donors, regardless of percentage. Ratios requiring balance sheet data (Current Ratio, Debt to Assets, Net Asset Ratio) are excluded from the score when no balance sheet is available.

Total Revenue?
Total Revenue
All income received during the fiscal year — grants, donations, program fees, government contracts, investment income, and other sources combined.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Statement of Activities, Total Revenue line
990 Form 990, Part VIII, Line 12 (Total Revenue)
OTHER Income statement or internal P&L — top-line total
Total Expenses?
Total Expenses
All money spent during the fiscal year across program, management, and fundraising activities.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Statement of Activities, Total Expenses line
990 Form 990, Part IX, Line 25 (Total Functional Expenses)
OTHER Income statement or internal P&L — total expenses line
Total Assets?
Total Assets
Everything the organization owns or controls — cash, receivables, property, investments, and equipment at year-end.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Balance Sheet, Total Assets
990 Form 990, Part X, Line 16 (Total Assets)
OTHER Internal balance sheet — total assets line
Total Liabilities?
Total Liabilities
Everything the organization owes — accounts payable, deferred revenue, loans, and other obligations at year-end.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Balance Sheet, Total Liabilities
990 Form 990, Part X, Line 26 (Total Liabilities)
OTHER Internal balance sheet — total liabilities line
Cash & Cash Equivalents?
Cash & Cash Equivalents
Immediately accessible funds — checking, savings, money market accounts, and short-term instruments convertible to cash within 90 days.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Balance Sheet, Cash and Cash Equivalents
990 Form 990, Part X, Line 1 + Line 2
OTHER Internal balance sheet or bank statements at year-end
Current Assets?
Current Assets
Assets expected to be converted to cash within 12 months — includes cash, receivables, prepaid expenses, and short-term investments.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Balance Sheet, "Current Assets" subtotal
990 Form 990 does not label "current assets" — add Part X Lines 1–4 as an estimate
OTHER Internal balance sheet — current assets subtotal
Current Liabilities?
Current Liabilities
Obligations due within 12 months — accounts payable, accrued expenses, short-term debt, and deferred revenue.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Balance Sheet, "Current Liabilities" subtotal
990 Form 990 does not label "current liabilities" — use Part X Lines 17–18 as an estimate
OTHER Internal balance sheet — current liabilities subtotal
Program Service Expenses?
Program Service Expenses
Money spent directly delivering the mission — staff, supplies, and overhead tied to programs and services.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Statement of Functional Expenses, Program Services column total
990 Form 990, Part IX, Line 25, Column B
OTHER Internal expense report — program or direct service costs
Management & General Expenses?
Management & General Expenses
Overhead costs not tied to programs or fundraising — executive leadership, finance, HR, legal, IT, and general administration.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Statement of Functional Expenses, Management & General column total
990 Form 990, Part IX, Line 25, Column C
OTHER Internal expense report — admin or overhead cost category
Fundraising Expenses?
Fundraising Expenses
Costs specifically incurred to solicit donations and grants — events, direct mail, donor stewardship, and development staff time.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Statement of Functional Expenses, Fundraising column total
990 Form 990, Part IX, Line 25, Column D
OTHER Internal expense report — development or fundraising cost category
Total Contributions (Donations)?
Total Contributions
All philanthropic support received — individual donations, foundation grants, corporate giving, and government grants.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Statement of Activities, Contributions and Grants line
990 Form 990, Part VIII, Line 1h
OTHER Internal revenue report — donations and grants received
Largest Single Revenue Source?
Largest Single Revenue Source
The single largest revenue line item — one funder, one contract, or one revenue stream. Used to measure concentration risk.
Where to find it
BEST Audited financials — Schedule of Grants and Contributions, or notes listing major funders
990 Form 990, Part VIII — scan Lines 1a–1f to identify the largest single item
OTHER Ask the org directly: "What is your largest single source of revenue?"
Why this matters
A large revenue concentration isn't automatically a risk. One government contract or single major gift disappearing would hurt — but a broad base of many individual donors represents community support and gives the organization flexibility.
