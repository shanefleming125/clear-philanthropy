The problem

Most funders are making high-stakes decisions with incomplete information.

📊
Data without interpretation
A 990 tells you what happened 18 months ago. Translating financial data into a funding decision — and communicating that decision back to the organization — requires expertise most teams don't have in-house.
⚙️
No operational infrastructure
Many foundations — especially smaller family foundations, family offices, and corporate giving programs — are doing serious grantmaking without dedicated program staff to run it well.
🔇
The feedback gap
Organizations declined without explanation can't improve. The best funders give actionable guidance — but most don't have the language to frame financial concerns constructively.
We declined organizations based on their finances, but rarely gave feedback — because we didn't know how to translate the numbers into something we could share.
— Foundation Program Officer
