Financial Data — Current Year

Enter figures from your most recent completed fiscal year. All amounts in USD. Tap ? next to any field if you need help finding the number.

?
All money your organization received during the year — grants, donations, program fees, government funding, and any other income. This is your top-line total.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part VIII Line 12, or the top of your income statement labeled "Total Revenue."
?
Everything your organization spent during the year — staff, programs, rent, administration, and all other costs combined.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part IX Line 25, or the bottom of your income statement labeled "Total Expenses."
?
Everything your organization owns — cash, bank accounts, investments, equipment, and property, as of your fiscal year end.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part X Line 16, or your balance sheet labeled "Total Assets."
?
Everything your organization owes — unpaid bills, loans, deferred revenue, and any other obligations as of your fiscal year end.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part X Line 26, or your balance sheet labeled "Total Liabilities."
?
Money your organization can access immediately — checking accounts, savings accounts, and money market funds. Does not include investments or receivables.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part X Lines 1–2, or the top of your balance sheet under "Cash and Cash Equivalents."
?
Assets you expect to convert to cash within the next 12 months — includes cash, money owed to you (receivables), and prepaid expenses. If you're not sure, use your cash figure as an estimate.
Where to find it: Your audited balance sheet under "Current Assets." The Form 990 doesn't label these separately.
?
Bills and obligations due within the next 12 months — accounts payable, accrued payroll, and short-term debt. If you're unsure, use your accounts payable figure as an estimate.
Where to find it: Your audited balance sheet under "Current Liabilities." The Form 990 doesn't label these separately.
?
Money spent directly delivering your programs and services — staff time, supplies, and costs tied to your mission. Does not include admin or fundraising.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part IX Line 25 Column B, or your income statement under "Program Expenses."
?
Overhead costs that keep the organization running — executive staff, finance, HR, IT, and general administration. Not directly tied to programs.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part IX Line 25 Column C, or your income statement under "Management and General."
?
Money spent raising money — events, mailings, grant writing staff time, and donor outreach. If your organization does no formal fundraising, enter 0.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part IX Line 25 Column D, or your income statement under "Fundraising Expenses."
?
All philanthropic support received — individual donations, foundation grants, corporate gifts, and government grants. Does not include earned revenue like program fees or service contracts.
Where to find it: Form 990 Part VIII Line 1h, or your income statement under "Contributions and Grants."
?
The single largest source of revenue in the year — one funder, one government contract, or one revenue stream. For example, if your biggest grant was $150,000 from a foundation, enter that amount.
Where to find it: Review your revenue sources and identify the largest single one. You may need to look at your grant records or income statement line by line.
